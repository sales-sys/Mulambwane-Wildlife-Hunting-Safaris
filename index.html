Sacred Province of Africa

Limpopo: Land of Legends

Where ancient baobab trees whisper ancestral wisdom and wildlife corridors connect sacred lands. This is Limpopo—the beating heart of Africa's heritage.

Ancient Baobab Trees

Home to some of Africa's oldest baobab trees, including specimens over 1,000 years old. These "Trees of Life" are sacred to our ancestors and serve as natural landmarks across our vast wilderness.

Rich Cultural Heritage

Limpopo is home to diverse African cultures including Venda, Tsonga, Pedi, and Ndebele peoples. Our guides share traditional knowledge, stories, and ancient tracking techniques passed down through generations.

Wildlife Migration Route

Located along ancient animal migration corridors connecting Kruger National Park with Zimbabwe and Botswana. This strategic position makes Mulambwane a vital wildlife sanctuary and exceptional safari destination.

Traditional Healing & Medicinal Plants

Limpopo Province is renowned as the "pharmacy of Africa," home to over 3,000 medicinal plant species. Our guided bush walks include educational sessions about traditional healing plants used by our ancestors for centuries.

  • • African Potato (Hypoxis hemerocallidea)
  • • Sutherlandia (Cancer Bush)
  • • Marula Tree (Sclerocarya birrea)
  • • Wild Fig Trees (Ficus sycamorus)

Traditional Knowledge Sharing

Experience authentic cultural exchange as our local guides, many of whom are traditional healers (sangomas), share ancestral knowledge about the medicinal and spiritual properties of indigenous plants.

Professional Conservation Philosophy

Excellence - Sacred Respect

For generations, the people of Limpopo Province have practiced respectful hunting that honors the sacred balance between humanity and nature. This time-tested wisdom guides every hunt at Mulambwane.

Our ethical hunting philosophy teaches that we honor and respect all wildlife. Every ethical hunt directly funds anti-poaching operations, habitat restoration, and conservation programs while maintaining healthy wildlife populations through scientific management.

As a Community Property Association (CPA), 70% of hunting proceeds stay within the Mulambwane community, creating sustainable livelihoods and incentivizing wildlife protection through proven stewardship practices.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to common questions about Mulambwane Safaris.

How do I book a safari?

You can send us an inquiry using the contact form above, email us directly, or call our bookings number. We will guide you through the process and help tailor your experience.

What is included in a safari package?

All packages include accommodation, guided activities, and transfers within the reserve. Specific inclusions depend on your chosen safari type.

Is Mulambwane suitable for families?

Absolutely! We offer family suites and child-friendly activities. Please mention your family requirements in your inquiry.

Do you cater for special dietary needs?

Yes, our kitchen can accommodate most dietary requirements. Please let us know your needs when booking.

How do I get to Mulambwane?
